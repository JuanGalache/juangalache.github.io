Nutri-Eat: My thesis project


An app designed for my thesis work. Its aim is to help people to spend effectively their time in the kitchen and teach what to cook in order to have a healthy nourishment.

Ideation Process

The premise of the thesis was to think about the future of healthy nourishment and make it profitable for Peabody. But to be able to develop a future scenario, it was important to first understand what does it means to have a healthy dieting. Using UX Research, I conducted interviews to experts like nutritionists and made a survey to gather information and understand the user's daily challenges about having a healthy dieting. After the research process and cross-checking information, I came out with a clear and concise definition about what it means to "eat healthy" and a high understanding of the people's pain points on their desire to carry out a healthy nourishment.

Using my Product, Service and UX/UI Design Skills, my proposal was the creation of an app that helps the user to develop a healthy habit by implementing a series of short term benefits that encourages the user to be healthy by utilizing the culinary appliances of Peabody.








The idea of Nutri Eat arises from the user's need to achieve a healthy nourishment but without having the necessary knowledge to carry it out. The app's purpose is to help the user to form a healthy habit in the kitchen by suggesting what the user can prepare with the ingredients available.







Nutri-Eat has 5 substancial parts. 1: The main page where you can see all the healthy food you can prepare and a pricing system. 2: The list where you get the ingredients needed for the food chosen. 3: The NEST Camera which allows you to scan all the ingredients and give you recommendations of what to eat. 4: The calendar which gives you an organized eating schelude to help you to be healthy. 5: The account where you can keep track of your process.
